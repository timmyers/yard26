## Project Overview

Yard planning and landscape design project for "Yard 26." This repo tracks design plans, plant selections, materials, and installation guides for various yard improvement projects.

## Current Projects

### Foundation Bed (house wall corner)
- **File:** `foundation-bed-design.html` — self-contained HTML design document with SVG layout diagram
- **Photos:** `foundation-bed-initial-status.jpg` — initial condition (March 2026); `foundation-bed-vision-year1.png` — AI-generated Year 1 vision rendering
- **Address:** 9736 Whitecliff Pl, Highlands Ranch, CO 80129
- **GPS:** 39.5391°N, 105.0158°W — elevation 5,900 ft
- **Dimensions:** 28 × 84 in (2.3 × 7 ft) = 16.3 sq ft — measured on-site
- **Location:** Foundation bed in house wall corner — green siding forms L-shape; long wall (84", faces 147° SSE) along left, short wall (28", faces 237° WSW) across top. Stairs at upper-right, sidewalk and walkway along right and bottom
- **Orientation:** South-facing — open arc spans 147°–237° azimuth (SSE to WSW), centered on ~192° (nearly due south)
- **Exposure:** ~5.5 hrs midday sun (summer peak), ~4.5 hrs at equinox — borderline full sun. Morning and late afternoon blocked by house walls. Midday sun is highest-intensity. 18% UV boost at elevation.
- **Climate:** Colorado Front Range, Zone 5b
- **Budget:** Under $200 (estimated $77–130 for materials)
- **Design philosophy:** Native biodiversity pocket garden — every plant chosen for ecological relationships, not just aesthetics. Intentional bare soil patches for ground-nesting bees.
- **Microclimate:** Foundation heat island (effective Zone 6a-6b), rain shadow from eaves (40-60% less precip), SSE-facing long wall reflects midday sun/heat into bed
- **Design:** Corner cluster (Milkweed back + Lead Plant front), center accent zone (Blanket Flower + Pasque Flower + rock + bare soil), stairs anchor (Anise Hyssop). Pussytoes groundcover along full front edge. Flat sandstone pieces leaned against exposed concrete foundation to soften appearance. Four ground surface types (decomposed granite, native stone, bare sandy soil, living groundcover)
- **Plants (7 acquired April 2026):** Dwarf Leadplant (*Amorpha nana*, corner-front structural anchor), Antelope Horns Milkweed (*Asclepias asperula*, corner-back monarch host — substituted for *A. tuberosa*), Purple Coneflower (*Echinacea purpurea*, stairs anchor — substituted for Anise Hyssop; needs slightly more water), Blanket Flower (*Gaillardia aristata*, center longest bloomer), Prairie Smoke (*Geum triflorum*, added — early bloomer with iconic feathery seed heads), Small-leaf Pussytoes (*Antennaria parvifolia*, front edge groundcover, will spread by stolons), European Pasqueflower (*Pulsatilla vulgaris* — Eurasian sister to native *P. patens*, near-identical visually/functionally)
- **Key rule:** No pesticides, no cultivars — true native species only (final list includes 2 non-strict-CO-natives: *E. purpurea* Great Plains, *P. vulgaris* Eurasian — both pollinator-friendly). Source from native nurseries
- **Foundation protection:** 4-6" clearance soil-to-siding, grade 2%+ away from house, DG (not organic mulch) against wall

## Conventions

- Design documents are self-contained HTML files with inline CSS and SVG diagrams
- Plant selections are 100% Colorado Front Range natives (Zones 4–5), drought-tolerant, chosen for ecological value
- Budget estimates use ranges to account for local nursery pricing
